    Neuro Scutbook¶

    A bedside neurology reference for MDs, NPs, PAs, and medics — how to work up and manage a patient with neurological symptoms in the clinic, ER, or on the wards, the way a neurology consult would. Every topic pairs a prescriptive consult workflow with a plain-language evidence brief and a standardized military-medicine layer.

    Disclaimer — read first

    This site is educational clinical decision-support. It is not a substitute for professional clinical judgment, formal consultation, or individual medical advice, and it does not establish a provider–patient relationship. Dosing, protocols, and military standards change — verify everything against current guidelines, product labeling, and the governing regulation before acting on it. Use reflects your own professional responsibility.

    How to use this¶

    • On call or at the bedside? Open the topic and read top-to-bottom: red flags → rapid approach → workup → tiered Step 1→2→3 order sets with doses → disposition.
    • Prescribing? Look for the TRICARE box wherever drugs appear — preferred versus PA-gated, and what the authorization needs.
    • Studying or teaching? Jump to the Discover brief at the bottom of each page for the evidence and the key trials.
    • Search — press / anywhere and type a symptom, drug, or trial name.

    How current is this?

    Every page carries a visible Last reviewed stamp and a Verified footer that states what was checked against source, what is conference or press-release data rather than peer-reviewed, and what still needs confirmation before you act on it. Where a recommendation rests on observational rather than randomized evidence, the page says so.
