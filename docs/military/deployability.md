# Military medicine — deployability & MEB/IDES primer

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

A shared reference for the **Military medicine** box that appears on every clinical topic. This
explains the framework once; each topic page then applies it to that condition.

!!! danger "Verify before you counsel or document"
    Standards change and vary by service and mission. Treat this as an **orientation to the
    framework**, not the authority. Always confirm the specifics against the **current**
    governing publications for the member's service and duty:

    - **DoDI 6130.03 — Medical Standards for Military Service** (joint, all-Service). Two volumes:
      **Vol 1 = Appointment, Enlistment, or Induction (accession)** and **Vol 2 = Retention.** The
      neurologic standards live in **para 6.26** (Vol 1) and **5.26** (Vol 2); vision/diplopia in
      **6.x / 5.4**; vestibular disease in **6.5** (Ears). These are the DoD floor that the Services
      implement.
    - **AR 40-501, *Standards of Medical Fitness*** — the Army's implementation. In the **27 June
      2019** edition, neurological retention standards are **para 3-31** (subpara **g** = migraine/
      tension/cluster headache; also MS, stroke, seizure, narcolepsy, and **k** = other neurologic
      residuals), vision/**binocular diplopia** is **para 3-7**, and aviation/special-duty is
      **Chapter 4** (head injury: table 4-2). *Where DoD says "frequent," the Army often supplies the
      number* (e.g., headaches ≥3 incapacitating days/month).
    - **DoDI 1332.18** and **DoD Manual 1332.18** — the Disability Evaluation System. **DoDM 1332.18
      Vol 1 (Processes, 2023)** and **Vol 2 (Quality Assurance, 2024)** govern how a member who fails
      a retention standard moves through the **DES** (the **IDES** integrates the VA exam).
    - Applicable **deployment medical standards / MOD** (e.g., CENTCOM MOD) for the theater.
    - **Aeromedical policy** (Aeromedical Policy Letters / service equivalents) for flight and
      other special-duty status.

## The five questions (the Military box)

Every clinical page answers these in order:

1. **Deployability** — Is the condition, or its required treatment/monitoring, compatible with the
   operational environment? Consider medications that need refrigeration, labs, controlled
   substances, or that impair heat tolerance, alertness, or judgment.
2. **Profile (DA Form 3349)** — Does it warrant a **temporary** profile (expected to resolve) or a
   **permanent** profile (stable limitation)? What are the specific functional limitations and
   duty restrictions?
3. **EPTS / Line of Duty** — Did it **exist prior to service** or was it **incurred/aggravated in
   the line of duty**? Document onset, pre-service history, and any service aggravation.
4. **Retention (AR 40-501)** — Does severity/frequency/treatment-response cross a threshold where
   the member no longer meets **medical retention standards**, triggering board referral?
5. **MEB / IDES** — When and how to refer: what documentation actually carries a board
   (objective frequency/severity, failed adequate treatment trials, and concrete duty impact).

## Process orientation (high level)

*AR 40-501 refers to sending a Soldier who fails a retention standard to **the DES (Disability
Evaluation System)** — the umbrella for the MEB → PEB sequence below, which the **IDES** integrates
with the VA disability exam.*

- **MEB (Medical Evaluation Board):** documents the condition(s) and whether the member meets
  retention standards.
- **PEB (Physical Evaluation Board):** determines fitness for continued service and, if unfit,
  disability disposition.
- **IDES:** the integrated DoD/VA process linking the above with VA disability evaluation.

## Documentation that carries a board

Regardless of condition, boards are moved by **objective, longitudinal, duty-relevant** evidence:

- Frequency/severity captured over time (diaries, encounter logs, ER visits).
- **Adequate** treatment trials that were tried and failed (drug, dose, duration).
- Specific **duty impact** — missed training, non-deployability, work restrictions.
- Consistent profiles and a clear EPTS/LOD determination.
