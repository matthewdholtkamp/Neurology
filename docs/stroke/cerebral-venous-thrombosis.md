# Cerebral venous sinus thrombosis (CVST)

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

**One-liner:** thrombosis of the dural venous sinuses or cortical veins — the **great mimic and the
great miss.** It hits **young people (often women on estrogen or peripartum)**, presents most often
as a **new, worsening headache**, and the counter-intuitive rule that saves brains is: **you
anticoagulate even when there's hemorrhage.**

!!! danger "🚩 Think CVST when the headache doesn't fit an artery"
    Suspect it with:

    - **New/progressive or thunderclap headache** — especially with **papilledema, seizures,
      encephalopathy, or focal deficits.**
    - A **hemorrhage or infarct that crosses arterial territories** (bilateral, parasagittal, or
      temporal — venous distribution).
    - **Isolated raised intracranial pressure** (headache + papilledema + diplopia mimicking IIH).
    - **Risk factors:** pregnancy/postpartum, **estrogen (OCP)**, thrombophilia, malignancy, head/neck
      infection (mastoiditis, sinusitis), dehydration, IBD.

    **Get dedicated venous imaging — MRV or CTV.** A "normal" non-contrast CT does **not** exclude it
    (look for a dense cord/sinus sign, or a bleed that doesn't fit an artery).

## Rapid approach

1. **Image the veins:** **MRV or CTV** (the diagnostic test — don't rely on non-contrast CT or a
   negative D-dimer).
2. **Anticoagulate — even if there's venous hemorrhage** (the bleed is *from* the clot; treating the
   clot helps).
3. **Treat complications:** seizures, raised ICP / threatened vision, large venous infarct.
4. **Find the cause:** pregnancy/estrogen, thrombophilia, infection, malignancy.
5. **Plan duration** of anticoagulation and counsel on estrogen avoidance / future pregnancy.

## The prescriptive flow (what I want done, in order)

1. **Confirm on MRV/CTV** (± MRI showing venous infarct/hemorrhage).
2. **Start therapeutic anticoagulation now** — **LMWH** preferred (even with hemorrhage present).
3. **Admit; treat seizures and raised ICP;** neurosurgery on standby for a large infarct.
4. **Transition to oral anticoagulation** and **set the duration** by provoked vs unprovoked/thrombophilia.
5. **Work up etiology**; **stop estrogen**; counsel future-pregnancy prophylaxis.

## Acute treatment

!!! orderset "Anticoagulate — yes, even with hemorrhage"
    - **Therapeutic LMWH** (e.g., enoxaparin 1 mg/kg SC q12h) is **first-line acutely** — preferred
      over unfractionated heparin (better outcomes) unless renal failure or likely need for rapid
      reversal/procedures favors **UFH infusion**.
    - **Intracranial hemorrhage from the CVST is NOT a contraindication** — the venous congestion that
      caused the bleed improves with anticoagulation. This is the point everyone finds
      counter-intuitive; do it anyway.
    - **Endovascular thrombectomy/thrombolysis is NOT routine** — **TO-ACT** was negative. Reserve it
      for **clinical deterioration despite adequate anticoagulation.**

!!! orderset "Manage the complications"
    - **Seizures:** treat clinical seizures; **give an ASM if there's a supratentorial lesion +
      seizure** (high recurrence). No routine prophylaxis without a lesion/seizure.
    - **Raised ICP / papilledema threatening vision:** **acetazolamide**, therapeutic **LP**, monitor
      visual fields/acuity; shunt or optic-nerve-sheath fenestration if vision is failing.
    - **Large venous infarct with mass effect / herniation:** **decompressive surgery is
      life-saving** — escalate early, don't just watch.

## Transition & duration

!!! orderset "Oral anticoagulation and how long"
    - **Transition to an oral anticoagulant** once stable. **Dabigatran** (RE-SPECT CVT: non-inferior
      to warfarin, less bleeding) and **rivaroxaban** are reasonable **DOAC** options for
      **non-VITT, non-antiphospholipid** CVST; **warfarin** where a DOAC isn't suitable (e.g.,
      antiphospholipid syndrome).
    - **Duration:**
        - **Provoked (transient factor, e.g., estrogen):** **3–6 months.**
        - **Unprovoked:** **6–12 months.**
        - **Recurrent VTE or severe thrombophilia / antiphospholipid:** **indefinite.**
    - **Stop estrogen-containing contraception permanently;** in future pregnancies, give **LMWH
      prophylaxis** through pregnancy and postpartum (highest-risk window).

!!! tip "TRICARE — CVST prescribing"
    **Enoxaparin (LMWH), warfarin, and acetazolamide are generic/formulary.** **DOACs** are covered —
    a **preferred DOAC** exists on the Uniform Formulary (dabigatran has the best CVST-specific
    evidence via RE-SPECT CVT). *Verify the current preferred agent and any PA at the TRICARE
    Formulary Search.*

## Disposition

Admit (stroke unit / ICU if depressed consciousness, large infarct, or refractory seizures/ICP).
Neurosurgery available for decompression. Outpatient hematology/thrombophilia work-up and
anticoagulation follow-up; repeat venous imaging to document recanalization.

!!! military "Military medicine — cerebral venous thrombosis"
    *Framework — verify against current **AR 40-501**, **DoDI 6130.03**, **DoDI 1332.18**, and
    aeromedical policy before counseling.*

    - **Demographics fit the force:** young service members, **estrogen contraception**, dehydration
      (field/heat), and peripartum are all in play — keep CVST on the differential for a service
      member with a new persistent headache and papilledema.
    - **Deployability:** acute CVST and the anticoagulation that follows are **deployment-limiting**;
      **estrogen contraception should be changed** after CVST.
    - **Retention / MEB:** residual deficit, seizures, or a thrombophilia requiring **indefinite
      anticoagulation** generally triggers the **DES (MEB/PEB)**. Document etiology, imaging,
      anticoagulation plan, and duration.
    - **Aeromedical:** CVST history, seizures, and chronic anticoagulation are **disqualifying/
      waiver-dependent** for flying duty.

## 📚 Discover — evidence brief

- **Anticoagulate through the hemorrhage.** The venous bleed is a consequence of the clot;
  therapeutic **LMWH** (preferred over UFH) is first-line **even when ICH is present** — the single
  most counter-intuitive, most important teaching point.
- **Diagnose with venous imaging (MRV/CTV).** Non-contrast CT and D-dimer miss it; don't be
  reassured by either.
- **DOACs are now reasonable** for non-VITT/non-antiphospholipid CVST — **dabigatran** (RE-SPECT CVT)
  is non-inferior to warfarin with less bleeding.
- **Endovascular therapy isn't routine** — **TO-ACT** negative; reserve for deterioration despite
  anticoagulation. **Decompressive surgery is life-saving** for a herniating venous infarct.
- **Chase the cause and set the duration** — stop estrogen, screen for thrombophilia/malignancy, and
  anticoagulate 3–12 months (indefinite for high-risk thrombophilia).

**Key sources:** AHA/ASA CVT scientific statement; ESO CVT guideline; RE-SPECT CVT (dabigatran);
TO-ACT (endovascular); SECRET (rivaroxaban feasibility).
