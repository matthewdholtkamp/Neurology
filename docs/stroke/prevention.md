# Stroke prevention — primary & secondary

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

**One-liner:** two populations, one playbook run at different intensities — **primary** prevention
(never had a stroke) and **secondary** prevention (prior ischemic stroke or TIA). Both are mostly
relentless **risk-factor control**; **hypertension is the single highest-yield lever** in either
column. Use the tabs below.

!!! danger "The three things that move the needle most"
    1. **Blood pressure** — the #1 modifiable risk factor for stroke, primary *and* secondary.
    2. **Antithrombotic matched to mechanism** — antiplatelet vs anticoagulant (get this right).
    3. **High-intensity statin to a low LDL** — especially after an atherosclerotic event.

    Everything else (glucose, weight, tobacco, diet, activity, AF detection) matters — but if you
    only nail three things, nail these.

=== "Primary prevention (no prior stroke/TIA)"

    **Who:** anyone without a prior stroke/TIA — the goal is to prevent the first one. Anchored to the
    **2024 AHA/ASA Guideline for the Primary Prevention of Stroke** (first update since 2014).

    **The prescriptive flow (what I want done, in order):**

    1. **Estimate risk** and set the frame with **Life's Essential 8** (BP, lipids, glucose, weight,
       diet, activity, sleep, tobacco).
    2. **Blood pressure to <130/80** — lifestyle + drugs. The biggest single lever.
    3. **Statin by ASCVD risk** (see lipids row).
    4. **Screen for and anticoagulate AF** by CHA₂DS₂-VASc.
    5. **Treat diabetes/obesity** — and use agents with proven stroke benefit (**GLP-1 RA**).
    6. **Lifestyle** — Mediterranean diet, activity, tobacco cessation, alcohol moderation.
    7. **Screen women and address social determinants** (below).

    | Risk factor | Target / action | Notes |
    |---|---|---|
    | **Hypertension** | **<130/80** (stage 2, or stage 1 with high ASCVD risk) | Lifestyle + antihypertensive; the dominant modifiable risk |
    | **Lipids** | **Statin** per 10-yr ASCVD risk; healthy LDL | Higher-risk → high-intensity |
    | **Diabetes + high CV risk / CVD (A1c ≥7%)** | **GLP-1 RA** (semaglutide, dulaglutide) — **Class 1** | SUSTAIN-6 / REWIND showed stroke reduction; **SGLT2i** for HF/CKD |
    | **Atrial fibrillation** | **Anticoagulate (DOAC)** if **CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥2 (men) / ≥3 (women)** | DOAC over warfarin for non-valvular AF |
    | **Overweight/obesity** | Weight loss; consider GLP-1 | |
    | **Tobacco** | Cessation (counseling + pharmacotherapy) | |
    | **Diet** | **Mediterranean diet + nuts/olive oil** (PREDIMED) | |
    | **Physical activity** | ≥150 min/wk moderate | Reduce sedentary time |
    | **Alcohol** | Limit (no "cardioprotective" license) | |

    !!! warning "Aspirin is NOT for routine primary prevention"
        **Do not start aspirin to prevent a first stroke in most people** — ASPREE, ARRIVE, and
        ASCEND showed the bleeding risk offsets the benefit. Specifically **not recommended** in
        diabetes, CKD, or adults **>70** with CV risk factors. Reserve low-dose aspirin for narrowly
        selected high-ASCVD / low-bleeding-risk patients after a shared decision. **Exception:** in
        pregnancy, **low-dose aspirin reduces preeclampsia** in high-risk individuals (see below).

    !!! tip "Women-specific prevention (new emphasis, 2024)"
        - **Screen everyone capable of pregnancy** for modifiable risks, medical conditions, and
          **social determinants** (food insecurity, transportation).
        - **Adverse pregnancy outcomes — preeclampsia, gestational HTN/diabetes, preterm birth —
          raise lifetime stroke risk;** document them and manage vascular risk long-term.
        - **Treat verified BP ≥160/110 in pregnancy or within 6 weeks postpartum;** give **low-dose
          aspirin** to high-risk pregnancies to prevent preeclampsia.
        - **Endometriosis, premature ovarian insufficiency, and early menopause** are risk enhancers.

    !!! tip "Social determinants of health"
        **Screen for and address adverse SDOH** wherever at-risk patients are seen — access, food
        security, transportation, and cost drive risk-factor control and are part of the guideline.

=== "Secondary prevention (prior ischemic stroke/TIA)"

    **Who:** anyone with a prior ischemic stroke or TIA — higher baseline risk, so **higher-intensity**
    control. **Start it before discharge.** Anchored to the **2021 AHA/ASA secondary-prevention
    guideline** and later trials.

    **The prescriptive flow (what I want done, in order):**

    1. **Define the mechanism** (cardioembolic vs large-artery vs small-vessel vs ESUS) — it dictates
       the antithrombotic.
    2. **Antithrombotic:** antiplatelet **or** anticoagulant (never reflexively both).
    3. **High-intensity statin → LDL <70** (± ezetimibe / PCSK9).
    4. **BP <130/80.**
    5. **Diabetes / obesity** — GLP-1 RA or SGLT2i where indicated.
    6. **Mechanism-specific procedure** (carotid, PFO, LAAO).
    7. **Lifestyle + adherence** (the intervention patients most often drop).

    !!! orderset "Every patient (start in hospital)"
        - **High-intensity statin — atorvastatin 80 mg** (or rosuvastatin 20–40); **target LDL <70**
          (TST); add **ezetimibe → PCSK9 inhibitor** to get there.
        - **BP <130/80** once stable; restart/optimize antihypertensives.
        - **Diabetes:** A1c control; **GLP-1 RA / SGLT2i** for their vascular benefit.
        - **Lifestyle:** Mediterranean diet, activity, tobacco cessation, alcohol moderation, weight.
        - **Adherence & follow-up** — reconcile meds; this is where secondary prevention fails.

    **Antithrombotic — matched to mechanism:**

    | Mechanism | Antithrombotic |
    |---|---|
    | **Non-cardioembolic (most)** | **Single antiplatelet** — aspirin, clopidogrel, or ASA-dipyridamole |
    | **Minor stroke (NIHSS ≤3) / high-risk TIA (ABCD² ≥4)** | **21-day DAPT** (ASA + clopidogrel), then single agent (CHANCE/POINT); **ticagrelor+ASA** (THALES) if CYP2C19 LOF |
    | **Symptomatic intracranial atherosclerosis** | **90-day DAPT** + aggressive risk-factor control (**SAMMPRIS**); avoid stenting first-line |
    | **Cardioembolic (AF)** | **DOAC** (apixaban/rivaroxaban/dabigatran/edoxaban) over warfarin |
    | **Symptomatic carotid 70–99% (or 50–69% selected men)** | **CEA or CAS within 2 weeks** + medical therapy |
    | **ESUS / cryptogenic** | Antiplatelet + **prolonged rhythm monitoring**; treat AF if found |
    | **PFO, age <60, otherwise-cryptogenic** | **PFO closure** (RESPECT/CLOSE/REDUCE) + antiplatelet |

    !!! tip "AF timing — start the DOAC early"
        Don't sit on the old rigid "1-3-6-12" wait. The **CATALYST** meta-analysis (2025) shows
        **early DOAC (≤4 days) reduces recurrence with no excess sICH.** Individualize the exact day
        on infarct size / hemorrhagic transformation (sooner for small strokes, a touch later for
        large infarcts). **LAAO** if anticoagulation is truly contraindicated.

    !!! warning "Don't double up without a reason"
        **Long-term DAPT is not secondary prevention** — after the 21-day (or 90-day ICAD) window,
        drop to a single agent; extended DAPT only adds bleeding. And **don't combine an antiplatelet
        with an anticoagulant** unless there's a separate, explicit indication.

## The full acute-stroke workup and reperfusion algorithm

For the **code-stroke** side — last-known-well, imaging, thrombolysis, and thrombectomy — see the
[acute ischemic stroke page](index.md). This page is the **prevention** companion; secondary
prevention is *started* during that admission and *owned* in clinic afterward.

!!! tip "TRICARE — prevention prescribing"
    - **Statins (atorvastatin/rosuvastatin), aspirin, and clopidogrel** are **generic/formulary,
      no PA** — the backbone of both columns.
    - **DOACs** are covered; a **preferred DOAC** exists on the Uniform Formulary — **verify the
      current preferred agent and any PA at the TRICARE Formulary Search.**
    - **GLP-1 RAs and PCSK9 inhibitors** are typically **PA-gated** (indication/step-therapy) — plan
      for the prior authorization.
    - **Ezetimibe** is generic/formulary and an easy add-on toward LDL <70.

!!! military "Military medicine — stroke prevention"
    *Framework — verify against current **AR 40-501**, **DoDI 6130.03**, and aeromedical policy.*

    - **Risk-factor control is a readiness issue:** untreated HTN, tobacco, and obesity drive both
      stroke risk and profile/PHA findings — treat aggressively.
    - **Anticoagulation is deployment- and duty-limiting:** AF on a DOAC, or a mechanical valve on
      warfarin, complicates deployment and many special-duty positions — factor it into MEB/profile
      discussions.
    - **Aeromedical:** anticoagulation and most post-stroke states are **disqualifying/waiver-
      dependent** for flying duty; confirm the standard for the class involved.
    - **Young service members:** don't anchor on "too young for stroke" — pursue dissection, PFO,
      hypercoagulable states, and substance use; the etiology drives the prevention plan.

## 📚 Discover — evidence brief

- **Primary prevention got its first refresh since 2014 (2024 AHA/ASA).** Headlines: **aspirin is
  out** for routine primary prevention (ASPREE/ARRIVE/ASCEND); **GLP-1 receptor agonists are in**
  (Class 1) for diabetes with high CV risk (SUSTAIN-6/REWIND); **BP <130/80**; the **Life's Essential
  8** framework; and new, explicit emphasis on **women's risk (adverse pregnancy outcomes)** and
  **social determinants of health.**
- **Secondary prevention is mechanism-driven.** Antiplatelet for non-cardioembolic (short DAPT only
  in the CHANCE/POINT window; 90-day DAPT for ICAD per SAMMPRIS), **DOAC for AF started early**
  (CATALYST), carotid revascularization for symptomatic high-grade stenosis, and PFO closure under 60.
- **Lipids: lower is better after atherosclerotic stroke** — high-intensity statin to **LDL <70**
  (TST), escalating with ezetimibe/PCSK9.
- **Blood pressure is the common denominator** — the highest-yield lever in both columns.

**Key sources:** 2024 AHA/ASA Primary Prevention of Stroke guideline; 2021 AHA/ASA Secondary
Prevention guideline; PREDIMED (Mediterranean diet); ASPREE/ARRIVE/ASCEND (aspirin); SUSTAIN-6/REWIND
(GLP-1); CHANCE/POINT/THALES (DAPT); SAMMPRIS (ICAD); CATALYST/ELAN (AF timing); TST (LDL target);
NASCET (carotid); RESPECT/CLOSE/REDUCE (PFO).
