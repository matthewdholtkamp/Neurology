# Transient ischemic attack (TIA)

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

**One-liner:** a **transient** focal neurologic deficit from brain/retinal ischemia **without
infarction** — a warning shot, not a benign event. The stroke that follows is **preventable**, and
the risk is **front-loaded to the first 48 hours**, so a TIA is a **same-day emergency work-up**, not
a "come back to clinic next week."

!!! danger "🚩 A TIA is a treated emergency — act in hours, not days"
    - **Definition is tissue-based:** transient deficit **with no infarction on imaging.** If MRI
      shows a matching **DWI lesion, it's a stroke** (treat/secondary-prevent as such), not a TIA.
    - **Highest recurrence is immediate** — a real TIA carries a substantial **90-day stroke risk,
      concentrated in the first 2 days.** Rapid work-up + prevention cuts that risk by **up to ~80%.**
    - **Exclude mimics:** seizure/Todd's, migraine aura (spreading, positive symptoms), hypoglycemia,
      syncope/pre-syncope, functional. TIA is **sudden, maximal, focal, negative** (loss of function).

## Rapid approach

1. **Confirm it's vascular and transient** (sudden, focal, negative symptoms; fully resolved).
2. **Risk-stratify** (ABCD² — below) to decide admit vs. expedited-outpatient.
3. **Same-day work-up** — MRI, vessel imaging, ECG/telemetry, echo (order set).
4. **Start secondary prevention immediately** — don't wait for the work-up to finish.
5. **Find the mechanism** and treat it (AF → anticoagulate; carotid → revascularize).

## Risk-stratify — ABCD² (and beyond)

| ABCD² factor | Points |
|---|---|
| **A**ge ≥60 | 1 |
| **B**P ≥140/90 at presentation | 1 |
| **C**linical: unilateral weakness **2** / speech disturbance without weakness **1** | 1–2 |
| **D**uration ≥60 min **2** / 10–59 min **1** | 1–2 |
| **D**iabetes | 1 |

*2-day stroke risk rises with score (~1% at 0–3, ~4% at 4–5, ~8% at 6–7). **ABCD²-I** adds carotid
stenosis ≥50% and a positive DWI (2 points each) — a positive DWI or a high score means **higher
risk → admit / urgent work-up.***

!!! warning "Don't let ABCD² alone gatekeep — it's a weak discriminator"
    ABCD² performs **sub-optimally** in validation (low sensitivity for "low-risk," low specificity for
    "high-risk") because it ignores **vascular mechanism and imaging.** **Never use a low score to send
    home a crescendo/recurrent TIA, a carotid/AF source, or a positive DWI** — those are high-risk
    regardless. Treat the **mechanism and the imaging**, not the number; newer tools (Canadian TIA
    Score) and, above all, **vessel + brain imaging** should drive disposition.

## The prescriptive flow (what I want done, in order)

1. **Confirm TIA vs stroke vs mimic**; get a **glucose** and a focused exam.
2. **Risk-stratify** (ABCD², dual/crescendo pattern, known AF/carotid disease).
3. **Complete the same-day work-up** (order set).
4. **Start antithrombotic + statin now** (below) — before results are all back.
5. **Mechanism-directed prevention:** AF → **early DOAC**; symptomatic carotid → **revascularize
   within 2 weeks**; ESUS → monitoring.
6. **Disposition:** admit high-risk; otherwise a **rapid-access TIA pathway** with prevention started.

## Same-day work-up

!!! orderset "Order it all today (a rapid TIA pathway cuts recurrent stroke ~80%)"
    - **MRI brain (DWI)** — up to **half of clinical "TIAs" show infarction** (reclassifies to stroke
      and changes prognosis). CT if MRI unavailable.
    - **Vessel imaging — CTA or MRA of head *and* neck** — find **symptomatic carotid stenosis** and
      dissection/intracranial disease.
    - **ECG + telemetry / prolonged monitoring** — hunt for **atrial fibrillation** (extend monitoring
      if cryptogenic).
    - **Echocardiography** for a cardioembolic source; **labs** (glucose, lipids, CBC, coags, A1c).

## Start secondary prevention immediately

!!! orderset "Antithrombotic + statin — start today (don't wait for the work-up)"
    - **High-risk TIA (ABCD² ≥4) or minor stroke: 21-day DAPT.** **Aspirin + clopidogrel** (clopidogrel
      300–600 mg load → 75 mg/day; ASA 81 mg) for **21 days**, then **single agent** — benefit is
      concentrated in the first 21 days (**CHANCE/POINT**); longer DAPT only adds bleeding.
        - **The window is wider than you think — up to 72 h.** **INSPIRES** (NEJM 2023) showed
          clopidogrel+aspirin started **within 72 h** of a minor stroke/high-risk TIA still cut 90-day
          stroke (7.3% vs 9.2%) — at the cost of more (still low) bleeding (0.9% vs 0.4%). So a patient
          presenting 1–3 days out **can still benefit.**
        - **Ticagrelor + aspirin** (THALES) is an alternative — and **preferred in CYP2C19
          loss-of-function** carriers (**CHANCE-2**, where the benefit was clearest in lower-risk
          patients), if genotyping is available.
    - **Lower-risk TIA:** **single antiplatelet** (aspirin, clopidogrel, or ASA-dipyridamole).
    - **High-intensity statin — atorvastatin 80 mg**, target **LDL <70**.
    - **Blood pressure** control toward **<130/80** once stable.
    - **Full detail + the mechanism-by-mechanism playbook: [Stroke prevention page](prevention.md).**

=== "Atrial fibrillation"
    **Anticoagulate — a DOAC** (over warfarin for non-valvular AF), and **start early** — after a
    *TIA* (no infarct) you can generally begin within a day or two (no waiting; CATALYST supports early
    initiation). **LAAO** if anticoagulation is contraindicated.

=== "Symptomatic carotid stenosis"
    **70–99% (or 50–69% in selected patients): revascularize within 2 weeks** (CEA first-line; CAS/
    TCAR in selected anatomy) + medical therapy — see the [carotid disease page](carotid-disease.md).

=== "ESUS / cryptogenic"
    Antiplatelet + **prolonged rhythm monitoring** (≥30-day/implantable) to catch occult AF; **PFO
    closure** in selected patients <60.

## Disposition

- **Admit / observe** with an expedited work-up for **high-risk** features: ABCD² ≥4, **crescendo or
  dual TIA**, known **AF or symptomatic carotid stenosis**, or a positive DWI.
- **Lower-risk** patients can go through a **rapid-access TIA clinic/pathway** — *only* if same-day
  imaging and prevention are actually available; otherwise observe.
- **Either way, secondary prevention starts at first contact.**

!!! military "Military medicine — TIA"
    *Framework — verify against current **AR 40-501**, **DoDI 6130.03**, and aeromedical policy.*

    - **Retention hinges on the work-up.** **DoDI 6130.03 Vol 2 para 5.26.b(6)** places **recurrent
      TIA below retention standards *unless the underlying etiology is identified and definitively
      treated*** (gated by 5.26.a); Army implements at **AR 40-501 para 3-31f.** So a **single TIA with
      a fully worked-up, definitively-treated cause** (e.g., carotid revascularized, AF anticoagulated)
      can retain — an **unexplained or recurrent** TIA trends toward the **DES (MEB/PEB).** Document
      the etiology work-up, the treatment, and duty impact.
    - **Deployability:** non-deployable during work-up; a treated single-cause TIA may return to duty
      per specialty review; **anticoagulation** for AF is itself deployment/special-duty-limiting.
    - **Aeromedical:** a history of TIA is **disqualifying/waiver-dependent** for flying duty.

## 📚 Discover — evidence brief

- **TIA is defined by tissue, not time** — transient deficit *without* infarction; a DWI lesion makes
  it a stroke. **~Half of clinical TIAs are actually infarcts on MRI.**
- **The danger is immediate** — highest stroke risk in the first 48 h, so **same-day imaging + started
  prevention**; rapid TIA pathways cut recurrent stroke **~80%.**
- **21-day DAPT** for high-risk TIA/minor stroke (CHANCE/POINT), then single agent — **and the window
  extends to 72 h** (INSPIRES), so a patient seen days out still benefits; ticagrelor+ASA / CHANCE-2
  for CYP2C19 LOF. Add **high-intensity statin to LDL <70**; **BP <130/80.**
- **Don't over-trust ABCD²** — it's a weak discriminator; let **vessel + brain imaging and the
  mechanism** drive disposition, not the score.
- **Treat the mechanism** — early DOAC for AF, carotid revascularization within 2 weeks for
  symptomatic stenosis, monitoring for ESUS. Full detail on the **[prevention page](prevention.md).**
