# Intracerebral hemorrhage (ICH)

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

**One-liner:** spontaneous (non-traumatic) bleeding into the brain parenchyma — the **other half of
"CT shows blood vs no blood."** Higher early mortality than ischemic stroke, and the first hours are
a **bundle of parallel actions**: reverse anticoagulation, drop the blood pressure, get neurosurgery
on the phone, and protect against expansion. Do them **together**, not in sequence.

!!! danger "🚩 First 60 minutes — the ICH bundle (do these in parallel)"
    The **INTERACT3 care bundle** — started **within 1 hour** and held for 7 days — improves survival
    and function. Four moves, simultaneously:

    1. **Blood pressure → SBP <140** (but **not <130** — harmful). Smooth, sustained.
    2. **Reverse anticoagulation** to **INR <1.5** / agent-specific reversal (below).
    3. **Glucose** to target (6.1–7.8 non-diabetic; 7.8–10 diabetic mmol/L) and **temp <37.5 °C**.
    4. **Call neurosurgery now** — cerebellar bleed, hydrocephalus, or large lobar clot may need the OR.

    And immediately: **airway/GCS**, stop the causative drug, **repeat CT at ~6 h** (expansion is the
    enemy), elevate head of bed 30°, treat herniation.

## Rapid approach

1. **CT confirms blood** → this is ICH; note **location, volume, IVH, and midline shift**.
2. **Start the bundle** (BP, reversal, glucose, temp) within the hour.
3. **Reverse the specific anticoagulant** (below) — the single highest-yield drug decision.
4. **Neurosurgery consult** for cerebellar/large/hydrocephalus; **ICU** for everyone.
5. **Find the cause** — deep (hypertensive) vs lobar (CAA/secondary); image the vessels if atypical.
6. **Do not prognosticate early** — avoid new DNR/care limits in the first 24–48 h (self-fulfilling).

## Diagnosis & etiology (where the bleed is tells you why)

| Location | Usual cause |
|---|---|
| **Deep** — basal ganglia, thalamus, pons, cerebellum | **Hypertensive** (small-vessel/Charcot-Bouchard) |
| **Lobar** — cortical/subcortical, elderly | **Cerebral amyloid angiopathy (CAA)** |
| **Lobar, young/normotensive, or atypical** | **Secondary** — AVM, aneurysm, dural AV fistula, tumor, **CVST**, vasculitis, drugs (sympathomimetics) |

- **Image the vessels/lesion (CTA ± MRI/MRV)** in the young, normotensive, lobar, or atypical bleed —
  don't miss an operable AVM, aneurysm, tumor, or **venous** cause.
- **CTA "spot sign"** predicts hematoma expansion.

## The prescriptive flow (what I want done, in order)

1. **Confirm ICH on CT**; record volume/IVH/location.
2. **Run the INTERACT3 bundle** (BP <140, reversal, glucose, temp) within 1 h, hold 7 days.
3. **Reverse the anticoagulant** by agent (below).
4. **Neurosurgery** for cerebellar/hydrocephalus/large supratentorial; **EVD** for IVH/hydrocephalus.
5. **ICU supportive care** — ICP, DVT prophylaxis, glucose/temp, dysphagia, no routine seizure ppx.
6. **Work up the cause**; plan secondary prevention (BP is everything) and antithrombotic-resumption.

## Blood pressure

!!! orderset "BP target — SBP <140, and don't overshoot"
    - **Target SBP <140 within 1 h**, then **keep 130–150 for ≥7 days.** **Lowering below 130 is
      potentially harmful** — and **avoid big swings** (variability worsens outcome).
    - **Titratable IV agents:** **nicardipine** or **clevidipine** infusion (smooth control), or
      **labetalol** boluses. Use an arterial line for moderate/large bleeds.

## Anticoagulation & antiplatelet reversal

!!! orderset "Reverse by agent — the highest-yield decision in the room"
    | On this agent | Give |
    |---|---|
    | **Warfarin / VKA** | **4-factor PCC** (preferred over FFP — faster, lower volume) **+ IV vitamin K 10 mg** |
    | **Dabigatran** | **Idarucizumab 5 g IV** |
    | **Apixaban / rivaroxaban / edoxaban (factor Xa inhibitors)** | **Andexanet alfa** (ANNEXA-I: less hematoma expansion, **but more thrombotic events** — weigh it) **or 4-factor PCC** as the guideline alternative |
    | **Unfractionated / LMW heparin** | **Protamine** |
    | **Antiplatelet (aspirin/clopidogrel)** | **Do NOT transfuse platelets** — PATCH showed **harm** — unless a neurosurgical procedure is planned; DDAVP may be considered |

    Recheck coagulation after reversal; **goal INR <1.5.** Recheck a **CT at ~6 h** for expansion.

## Surgery

!!! orderset "Neurosurgical decisions"
    - **Cerebellar hemorrhage >3 cm, or with brainstem compression / hydrocephalus → emergent
      surgical evacuation** (Class 1). **Do not** temporize these with an EVD alone.
    - **Hydrocephalus / large IVH → external ventricular drain (EVD).**
    - **Supratentorial lobar or anterior basal-ganglia clot, ~30–80 mL, presenting early:**
      **minimally invasive evacuation** improved outcomes in **ENRICH** (2024) — refer to a center
      that does trans-sulcal parafascicular surgery. (Routine **open craniotomy is not** beneficial
      for most supratentorial ICH — STICH/STICH II negative; MISTIE III neutral.)
    - **Deteriorating / herniating:** osmotherapy (hypertonic saline or mannitol), head up, treat
      the cause; decompression is a salvage option.

## Supportive care

!!! orderset "ICU bundle"
    - **Glucose** to target; **temperature <37.5 °C**; euvolemia with isotonic fluids (no hypotonic).
    - **Seizures:** treat clinical/electrographic seizures; **no routine prophylaxis.** cEEG if
      unexplained depressed consciousness.
    - **VTE prophylaxis:** **intermittent pneumatic compression from day 1**; add **pharmacologic
      prophylaxis after 24–48 h** once the bleed is stable.
    - **Dysphagia screen before PO**; head of bed 30°; bowel/skin care; avoid steroids.
    - **Don't limit care early** — prognostic pessimism in the first 24–48 h is self-fulfilling; use
      the ICH score to communicate, not to withdraw.

## Secondary prevention & restarting antithrombotics

- **Blood pressure control is the dominant lever** — long-term **<130/80** prevents recurrence
  (deep/hypertensive bleeds especially).
- **CAA (lobar, elderly, microbleeds)** carries a **high re-bleed risk** — be cautious with any
  antithrombotic; avoid anticoagulation where possible.
- **Restarting anticoagulation after ICH (e.g., for AF):** individualize; if restarting, typically
  **defer ~4–8 weeks** and weigh the thrombotic indication against re-bleed risk (**LAAO** is an
  option when anticoagulation is too risky). Deep, hypertensive, well-controlled bleeds tolerate
  resumption better than lobar/CAA bleeds.

!!! tip "TRICARE — ICH prescribing"
    Acute reversal agents (**4F-PCC, idarucizumab, andexanet alfa, protamine, IV vitamin K**) are
    **inpatient/pharmacy-stocked**, not outpatient-formulary items — **andexanet is high-cost and
    often restricted**, so know your facility's stock and the 4F-PCC alternative. Outpatient
    **antihypertensives are generic/formulary.** *Verify agent availability with your inpatient
    pharmacy.*

## Disposition

**ICU / neuro-critical-care** for everyone; transfer to a center with **neurosurgery and EVD
capability** if you don't have it. Early rehab once stable.

!!! military "Military medicine — intracerebral hemorrhage"
    *Framework — verify against current **AR 40-501**, **DoDI 6130.03**, **DoDI 1332.18**, and the
    relevant **JTS neurosurgery/TBI CPGs** before counseling.*

    - **Deployed:** ICH forward is a **neurosurgical-evacuation** emergency — BP control, reversal if
      anticoagulated, airway, and **urgent MEDEVAC**; definitive care is rear.
    - **Young service member with spontaneous ICH:** **hunt for a secondary cause** (AVM, aneurysm,
      dAVF, CVST, sympathomimetic/stimulant use) — the etiology drives duty and the board.
    - **Deployability / retention:** ICH with residual deficit, seizures, or an untreated vascular
      lesion is **deployment-limiting** and generally triggers the **DES (MEB/PEB)**; document
      volume, deficit, etiology, and BP control.
    - **Aeromedical:** a history of ICH and most vascular lesions are **disqualifying/waiver-
      dependent** for flying duty.

## 📚 Discover — evidence brief

- **Care bundles beat single interventions.** **INTERACT3** (Lancet 2023) showed a goal-directed
  bundle — **early intensive BP lowering (<140), glucose and temperature control, and rapid
  anticoagulation reversal**, all within an hour and held 7 days — improved survival and function.
- **BP: aim <140, not lower.** Per the **2022 AHA/ASA guideline**, target SBP <140 (keep 130–150);
  **<130 is potentially harmful**, and **variability** matters — go smooth and sustained.
- **Reverse by agent.** 4F-PCC + vitamin K (VKA), idarucizumab (dabigatran), **andexanet alfa or
  4F-PCC** (factor Xa inhibitors — **ANNEXA-I**: less expansion, more thrombosis), protamine
  (heparin). **Don't transfuse platelets** for antiplatelet-associated ICH (**PATCH** = harm).
- **Surgery is selective.** **Cerebellar >3 cm / brainstem compression / hydrocephalus → operate.**
  **ENRICH** (2024) supports **minimally invasive** evacuation of 30–80 mL lobar/anterior
  basal-ganglia clots; routine open craniotomy (STICH/STICH II) and MISTIE III were not beneficial.
- **Don't call it early** — avoid early care limitation; prognostic nihilism is self-fulfilling.

**Key sources:** 2022 AHA/ASA spontaneous ICH guideline; INTERACT3 (bundle); ANNEXA-I (andexanet);
PATCH (platelets); ENRICH (minimally invasive surgery); STICH/STICH II, MISTIE III; ESO/EANS ICH
guideline.
