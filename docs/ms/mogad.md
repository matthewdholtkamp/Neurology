# MOG antibody-associated disease (MOGAD)

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

**One-liner:** a distinct antibody-mediated demyelinating disease targeting **myelin oligodendrocyte
glycoprotein** — neither MS nor NMOSD. It relapses less often, **recovers far better**, and is
notoriously **steroid-dependent**, so the management problem is usually the taper rather than the
attack.

**Who this is for:** anyone working up optic neuritis, myelitis, or ADEM — especially in children
and young adults, where MOGAD is substantially more common than NMOSD.

!!! danger "🚩 The three-way fork"
    Optic neuritis and myelitis have **three** antibody-defined destinations, and they diverge in
    treatment and prognosis. Send **both** cell-based assays every time.

    | | **MS** | **NMOSD (AQP4+)** | **MOGAD** |
    |---|---|---|---|
    | **Target** | Myelin (T-cell driven) | **Astrocyte** AQP4 | **Oligodendrocyte** MOG |
    | **Optic neuritis** | Unilateral, retrobulbar, good recovery | Severe, posterior/chiasmal, poor recovery | **Bilateral, anterior, marked disc swelling — but good recovery** |
    | **Myelitis** | Short segment, peripheral | **LETM**, central | LETM, often **conus involvement** |
    | **ADEM** | No | Rare | **Yes — the classic paediatric presentation** |
    | **Course** | Relapsing, then progressive | Relapsing, no progressive phase | **Often monophasic (~50%)**; no progressive phase |
    | **Recovery** | Variable | **Poor** | **Good** |
    | **CSF OCB** | **Positive ~85%** | Usually negative | Usually negative |
    | **Steroid response** | Good | Good, but needs PLEX | **Excellent — but relapses on withdrawal** |

## Diagnosis — 2023 international MOGAD panel criteria

The **2023 international MOGAD panel criteria** (Banwell et al., *Lancet Neurology*) require **all
three** of:

1. **A core clinical demyelinating event** — one of six:
    - Optic neuritis
    - Myelitis
    - **ADEM**
    - **Cerebral cortical encephalitis** (often with seizures — a MOGAD-specific presentation)
    - Brainstem or cerebellar deficit
    - Cerebral monofocal or polyfocal deficit
2. **Positive MOG-IgG** by cell-based assay
3. **Exclusion of alternative diagnoses**

!!! warning "Titre matters — this is where the criteria bite"
    The criteria weight the result by **titre**, because **low-positive MOG-IgG is common in people
    without MOGAD** and is the main source of misdiagnosis.

    - **Clear positive (high titre)** + a core event + exclusion → MOGAD.
    - **Low positive, borderline, or serum-only-positive-without-titre** → requires **additional
      supporting clinical or MRI features** before the diagnosis can be made.
    - **Never diagnose MOGAD on a low-positive antibody alone in an atypical syndrome.**

    Validation studies report **high performance in patients with a high pre-test probability**, but
    **modest specificity and positive predictive value** when applied broadly — which is exactly the
    over-testing trap. Send the assay when the syndrome fits, and interpret the titre, not just the
    sign.

    **Cell-based assay only.** And note that MOG-IgG can be **transiently positive after an ADEM-like
    illness** and then disappear — repeat testing at 6–12 months helps distinguish monophasic from
    relapsing disease.

## Features that suggest MOGAD

- **Bilateral simultaneous optic neuritis** with **prominent disc oedema** and perineural
  enhancement on orbital MRI — but with **good visual recovery**, which is what separates it from
  NMOSD.
- **ADEM** — particularly in children; MOGAD accounts for a large share of paediatric ADEM.
- **Cerebral cortical encephalitis with seizures** — FLAIR-hyperintense cortical swelling, often
  unilateral. Frequently misdiagnosed as encephalitis or a first unprovoked seizure.
- **Myelitis with conus medullaris involvement.**
- **Fluffy, poorly demarcated brain lesions** that **resolve substantially** on follow-up imaging —
  quite unlike the persistent, sharply defined lesions of MS.
- **Steroid dependence** — improvement on steroids, relapse as they are withdrawn. This pattern is
  strongly suggestive.

## The prescriptive flow (what I want done, in order)

1. **Send cell-based MOG-IgG and AQP4-IgG** on every optic neuritis, myelitis, ADEM, or cortical
   encephalitis.
2. **Interpret the titre**, not just positive/negative.
3. **Treat the attack** — steroids, with PLEX for severe or refractory presentations.
4. **Taper slowly.** This is the single most important management point in MOGAD.
5. **Decide whether it is monophasic or relapsing** — repeat serology at 6–12 months; persistent
   positivity predicts relapse.
6. **Start maintenance only for relapsing disease** — not after a single attack in a patient who
   seroconverts to negative.
7. **Military disposition** — profile, and referral under 3-31e.

## Acute attack treatment

!!! orderset "Step 1 — steroids, then taper slowly"
    - **Methylprednisolone 1000 mg IV daily × 3–5 days.**
    - **Then an oral taper over weeks to months — not days.**

    **This is the defining management point.** MOGAD is exquisitely steroid-responsive and equally
    prone to **rebound relapse on rapid withdrawal.** A three-day pulse with no taper, which is
    perfectly reasonable in MS, frequently produces a relapse within weeks in MOGAD.

    Taper over a **minimum of 3 months** after a significant attack, and longer if relapse occurred
    during a previous taper.

!!! orderset "Step 2 — severe or refractory attack"
    - **PLEX 5–7 exchanges** — for severe optic neuritis or dense myelitis, or steroid failure.
    - **IVIG 2 g/kg** — an effective alternative, and often preferred in children and in pregnancy.

## Maintenance therapy

!!! note "The landscape just changed — and it is not yet FDA-approved"
    **METEOROID**, the phase 3 trial of **satralizumab** (anti-IL-6 receptor) in MOGAD, met its
    primary endpoint: a **68% reduction in relapse risk** versus placebo, with **87% of treated
    patients relapse-free at 48 weeks versus 67% on placebo**, a **66% reduction in annualised
    relapse rate**, and onset of response as early as 8 weeks. Results were presented at the
    **2026 AAN Annual Meeting**.

    **This is the first successful phase 3 trial in MOGAD.** Satralizumab is FDA-approved for
    AQP4-seropositive NMOSD but **is not yet approved for MOGAD** — verify current regulatory
    status before counseling. **CosMOG**, evaluating **rozanolixizumab** (FcRn inhibitor), is
    also underway.

**Until an agent is approved, maintenance is off-label and evidence is observational:**

| Option | Notes |
|---|---|
| **IVIG** | Maintenance IVIG has the **most consistent observational support** in relapsing MOGAD, and is a common first choice — particularly in children |
| **Rituximab** | Effective in some, but **notably less reliable in MOGAD than in NMOSD** — breakthrough relapses on adequate B-cell depletion are well described. Do not assume NMOSD-level efficacy |
| **Mycophenolate mofetil** | Oral, reasonable maintenance option |
| **Azathioprine** | Slow onset; requires steroid bridging for months |
| **Chronic low-dose steroid** | Sometimes the pragmatic answer, with all the expected long-term costs |
| **Tocilizumab** | Anti-IL-6R; supportive observational data, and mechanistically aligned with the METEOROID result |

!!! warning "Who actually needs maintenance"
    **About half of MOGAD is monophasic.** Committing a patient to indefinite immunosuppression
    after a single attack over-treats a large fraction of them.

    Favour maintenance when: **relapses have occurred**, MOG-IgG **remains persistently positive**
    at 6–12 months, the attacks were **severe**, or relapse occurred **during a steroid taper**.
    A single attack with seroreversion to negative generally warrants observation, not immunotherapy.

!!! tip "TRICARE — MOGAD prescribing"
    Because no agent carries a MOGAD indication, maintenance therapy is **off-label by definition**,
    which makes prior authorisation harder rather than routine. Expect to submit clinical
    justification with serology, attack history, and the failed-taper narrative.

    **Maintenance IVIG** requires authorisation and is administered as a **medical benefit**;
    document the relapsing course and prior treatment explicitly. Oral agents (mycophenolate,
    azathioprine) are inexpensive generics and are the path of least resistance where clinically
    adequate. **Verify at the TRICARE Formulary Search**, and expect the off-label status to be the
    obstacle.

!!! military "Military medicine — MOGAD"
    *Framework — verify against current **AR 40-501**, **DoDI 6130.03 Vol 2**, and **DoDI 1332.18**.
    See [Profiles, MEB/MAR2 & deployability](../military/deployability.md).*

    - **Retention:** **AR 40-501 para 3-31e** — "multiple sclerosis, **optic neuritis, transverse
      myelitis**, and similar demyelinating disorders." MOGAD's two commonest presentations are
      named in the paragraph directly. DoD floor: **DoDI 6130.03 Vol 2 para 5.26.f**, gated by
      **5.26.a** (persists despite treatment **and** impairs duty). Cortical encephalitis with
      seizures additionally engages **5.26.c**, and any seizure disorder brings **3-31i** and
      **5.26.k** into play — see [seizures](../seizure/index.md).
    - **The disposition genuinely differs from MS and NMOSD, and this is worth arguing carefully.**
      MOGAD **recovers well and is monophasic about half the time.** A service member with a single
      attack, full recovery, seroreversion to negative, and no maintenance requirement may
      **legitimately meet retention standards** — 5.26.a requires that the condition *persist
      despite appropriate treatment* **and** impair duty, and a resolved monophasic illness does
      neither.
    - **So do not reflexively board a first MOGAD attack.** Establish whether the disease is
      relapsing before committing to a disposition. A **temporary profile** through the
      determination period, with repeat serology at 6–12 months, is often the correct action —
      and MRDP has not been reached until that question is answered.
    - **Refer to the DES when** the course proves **relapsing**, residual visual or motor deficit
      impairs duty, seizures occur, or the patient requires **ongoing immunosuppression or
      maintenance IVIG** — an infusion-dependent regimen is not deployable.
    - **Profile:** temporary during the attack and the monophasic-versus-relapsing determination;
      **permanent** once relapsing disease or fixed deficit is established.
    - **Aeromedical:** a demyelinating event is disqualifying for flying duty; a waiver discussion
      for a fully recovered monophasic case belongs with the aeromedical authority, not the
      treating neurologist.

## 📚 Discover — evidence brief

**Why MOGAD recovers when NMOSD does not.** MOG sits on the **outermost surface of the myelin
sheath and the oligodendrocyte membrane**, making it accessible to circulating antibody — but the
target is myelin itself, and the oligodendrocyte largely survives. Demyelination is followed by
genuine **remyelination**, which is why the fluffy MRI lesions of MOGAD often substantially resolve
and why vision recovers even after dramatic bilateral disc swelling. NMOSD, by contrast, destroys
**astrocytes** — a structural, complement-mediated necrosis that cavitates the cord and does not
repair. Two antibody-mediated demyelinating diseases, opposite prognoses, entirely because of what
the antibody binds.

**The nosological story.** MOGAD spent years as "seronegative NMOSD" or "atypical MS," and patients
were treated accordingly — often with agents that neither helped nor were needed. Its recognition as
a distinct entity, formalised by the 2023 panel criteria, is one of the clearer recent wins in
neuroimmunology. The 2026 METEOROID result marks its transition from a disease managed on
observational habit to one with randomised evidence.

**Key evidence:**

- **Banwell B, et al., *Lancet Neurology* 2023** — international MOGAD panel proposed criteria.
- **METEOROID (2026)** — satralizumab; **68% relapse-risk reduction**, the first positive phase 3
  in MOGAD.
- **CosMOG** — rozanolixizumab in relapsing MOGAD, ongoing.
- Validation cohorts of the 2023 criteria — strong performance at high pre-test probability,
  **modest specificity** when applied broadly.
