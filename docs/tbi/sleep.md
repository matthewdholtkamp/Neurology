# TBI comorbidity — Sleep

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** sleep disruption **sustains every other TBI symptom** (headache, mood, cognition,
fatigue) — **fix it first.** Insomnia, obstructive sleep apnea, and circadian disruption are all
common after TBI.

!!! orderset "Work it up and treat it"
    - **Insomnia → CBT-I is first-line.** Adjuncts: **melatonin**, **low-dose trazodone**.
      **Avoid chronic benzodiazepines / Z-drugs** — cognition, dependence, and fall/duty risk.
    - **Screen for obstructive sleep apnea** — common and under-recognized after TBI, and it drives
      "refractory" fatigue and cognitive complaints. **Order a sleep study** for snoring, witnessed
      apneas, or unexplained daytime sleepiness → **CPAP**.
    - **Circadian disruption** — enforce a **regular sleep–wake schedule**, morning light, evening
      screen/caffeine limits; relevant to shift work and deployment.
    - **Reassess** — improving sleep often unmasks how much of the "post-concussive" picture was
      sleep-driven.

*Military note:* untreated OSA and chronic hypnotics are duty/deployability considerations; document
the sleep work-up. See the [TBI hub](index.md) for the retention/aeromedical framing.
