# TBI comorbidity — Vestibular / dizziness

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** dizziness, imbalance, and motion sensitivity are common after TBI — and the highest-yield
move is to **catch the curable causes (BPPV)** and refer for **vestibular rehabilitation.**

!!! orderset "Work it up"
    - **Look for BPPV** — **Dix-Hallpike → Epley** repositioning. Common, curable, and frequently
      missed; check before attributing dizziness to "post-concussive syndrome."
    - **Vestibular rehabilitation therapy** for persistent dizziness/imbalance/oculomotor symptoms —
      it **facilitates return to activity** (cervical + vestibular PT: HR ~3.9 vs rest when symptoms
      persist >10 days).
    - **Distinguish the cause:** vestibular vs **orthostatic/autonomic** dizziness (see
      [Autonomic](autonomic.md)) vs a cervicogenic contribution — and, acutely, exclude a
      **[posterior-circulation stroke](../stroke/index.md) or dissection** (HINTS/red flags) before
      calling new dizziness "concussion."

*Military note:* persistent vestibular dysfunction is deployment- and (with the vision/oculomotor
overlap) special-duty-limiting — see the [TBI hub](index.md).
