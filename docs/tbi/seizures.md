# TBI comorbidity — Post-traumatic seizures

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** distinguish **early provoked seizures** from **post-traumatic epilepsy (PTE)**, and know
the one rule that trips people up — **prophylaxis is a 7-day intervention that does *not* prevent
epilepsy.**

## Know the risk — it stratifies sharply by severity

!!! note "Prevalence: early PTS vs post-traumatic epilepsy"
    - **Early PTS (≤7 days):** up to **15% in severe TBI** (down to **<5%** with prophylaxis); only
      **0–4% in mild/moderate** (mostly <2%).
    - **Subclinical (nonconvulsive) seizures:** **~25%** of moderate–severe TBI on continuous EEG —
      the main reason to monitor.
    - **Post-traumatic epilepsy (late, unprovoked):** **~⅓ of severe** TBI survivors; **4–15%** in
      moderate–severe; **45–53% in penetrating** injury; **mTBI roughly triples** baseline risk
      (absolute ~1–4%).
    - **10-year epilepsy risk** (Swedish registry, n≈112,000): **12.9%** focal cerebral, 8.1% diffuse,
      7.3% extracerebral, **2.6% mild** — vs **0.9%** in controls.
    - **Repeated TBI doubles it:** a Danish cohort (n≈2.5 M) found **HR 2.04 after a first** TBI and
      **HR 4.45 after a second**, staying elevated **for decades.**

!!! note "Risk factors for PTE (2024 ACS Best Practices) — who to watch long-term"
    Evacuated SDH, **contusion + SDH**, evacuated intraparenchymal hematoma; **GCS 3–8**; **early
    seizures** (especially delayed); **LOC/amnesia >1 day**; **>7 days to follow commands**; skull
    fracture, **unelevated depressed fracture, dural penetration**; nonreactive pupil(s), **parietal
    lesions**; **age >65, female sex, family history of epilepsy, history of depression.**

## Acute management

!!! orderset "Early-seizure prophylaxis — a 7-day intervention"
    - **Who gets it:** moderate–severe TBI **with intracranial bleeding / risk factors** → **7 days**
      of prophylaxis. **Not recommended** in TBI **without intracranial bleeding** or in **isolated
      traumatic SAH** (2024 ACS).
    - **Agent — levetiracetam is preferred:** **levetiracetam 500–1000 mg IV/PO q12h** (or ~20 mg/kg
      load) **or phenytoin** (20 mg/kg load then maintenance with levels). Meta-analyses show
      **equivalent efficacy** (OR ~0.85), but levetiracetam has **fewer interactions, no drug-level
      monitoring, and shorter ICU stays** — the 2026 Brain Trauma Foundation penetrating-TBI guideline
      names it the modern preferred agent.
    - **Start early, stop at 7 days.** AAN (Level A) — start ASAP after **severe** TBI to reduce early
      PTS; **do not continue beyond 7 days** (Level B).
    - **Mild/moderate TBI:** the benefit is small — a 2024 JAMA Neurology meta-analysis found an
      absolute risk reduction of **0.6% (NNT 167)** — weigh against ASM harms and the real risk of
      **inappropriate continuation** past 7 days.

!!! warning "The rule: prophylaxis does NOT prevent epilepsy"
    The landmark **Temkin RCT** showed phenytoin cut **early** PTS from **14.2% → 3.6%** but had **no
    effect on late PTS/PTE** — replicated across meta-analyses and the basis of every current guideline.
    **Early PTS are *associated* with later PTE**, but treating early seizures does **not** reduce PTE
    risk — so an early seizure should prompt **closer long-term surveillance**, **not** indefinite
    prophylaxis.

!!! warning "Continuous EEG — the 'won't wake up' after TBI"
    **Nonconvulsive seizures occur in ~25%** of moderate–severe TBI and are tied to **worse outcomes
    and hippocampal atrophy.** **Consider cEEG in any moderate–severe TBI with unexplained altered
    consciousness** ("won't wake up" → cEEG). The 2024 ACS guideline supports cEEG for seizure
    detection (and emerging detection of secondary ischemia); in the multicenter INTREPID study, **EEG
    background features** (absent posterior-dominant rhythm, absent sleep transients, predominant delta,
    discontinuous background) were **independently associated with functional outcome.**

!!! note "Penetrating TBI — highest risk, least certainty"
    **Penetrating TBI carries the highest PTE risk (45–53%)**, and **frontal/temporal** injuries are
    likely more epileptogenic. The 2026 Brain Trauma Foundation penetrating-TBI guideline flags pTBI as
    higher-risk than blunt but **did not reach consensus on prophylaxis *duration* or cessation
    criteria** — a genuine uncertainty for providers managing **combat-related penetrating injury.**

## Late seizures & follow-up

!!! orderset "Late (>7-day) unprovoked seizure = post-traumatic epilepsy"
    - A late **unprovoked** seizure means **epilepsy** — **start treatment and work it up.** Risk is
      highest after **penetrating or severe** TBI and can emerge **months–years** later.
    - **Driving (2025 AAN position statement):** exclusively **provoked** seizures from factors unlikely
      to recur may **not** need a seizure-free interval — but **sleep deprivation and alcohol
      withdrawal are *not* exceptions.** For unprovoked seizures the **minimum interval is ~3 months**,
      individualized by a medical advisory board.
    - **Full management** — the **status-epilepticus ladder**, first-seizure work-up, and ASM selection
      — is on the **[seizure & status epilepticus page](../seizure/index.md)** and the
      **[antiseizure medications page](../seizure/asm.md)**.

!!! note "Horizon — antiepileptogenesis (no proven therapy yet)"
    The key unmet need: **current ASMs prevent early seizures but do *not* modify epileptogenesis.** The
    2026 Epilepsia consensus roadmap (Zanier et al.) targets **neuroinflammation (IL-1β/NLRP3),
    blood-brain-barrier dysfunction, and mTOR signaling.** Preclinical candidates — **IL-1 receptor
    antagonist (anakinra), mTOR inhibitor (rapamycin), glutamate modulator (ceftriaxone)** — reduce
    seizures in animals but are **unvalidated in humans.** Candidate **biomarkers**: early **epileptiform
    EEG** (≈3× PTE risk within 14 days), serum **S100B** (HR ~2.12 in complicated mTBI), and
    neuroinflammatory markers. **Military relevance:** PTE is ~5% of all epilepsy and TBI is the
    signature military injury — moderate–severe/penetrating cohorts are a natural population for future
    prevention trials.

*Military note:* post-traumatic **epilepsy** is handled as any seizure disorder (AR 40-501 3-31i /
DoDI 6130.03 Vol 2 5.26.c → generally below retention; flying-duty disqualifying) — see the
[TBI hub](index.md) and [seizure page](../seizure/index.md).
