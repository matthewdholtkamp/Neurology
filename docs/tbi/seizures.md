# TBI comorbidity — Post-traumatic seizures

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** distinguish **early provoked seizures** from **post-traumatic epilepsy**, and know the
one rule that trips people up — **prophylaxis is a 7-day intervention that does *not* prevent epilepsy.**

!!! orderset "The two scenarios"
    - **Early post-traumatic seizure (≤7 days, provoked):** more common with moderate–severe TBI.
      **Prophylactic ASM — levetiracetam (or phenytoin) for ~7 days — reduces *early* seizures** in
      moderate–severe TBI. **It does NOT prevent later epilepsy** — so **don't continue it long-term
      for prophylaxis.**
    - **Late seizure (>7 days, unprovoked) = post-traumatic epilepsy** — risk is highest after
      **penetrating or severe TBI**, and it can emerge months–years later. A late unprovoked seizure
      means **epilepsy**: start treatment and work it up.

- **Full management** — the status-epilepticus ladder, first-seizure work-up, ASM selection, and
  driving/deployability rules — is on the **[seizure & status epilepticus page](../seizure/index.md)**
  and **[antiseizure medications page](../seizure/asm.md)**.

*Military note:* post-traumatic **epilepsy** is handled as any seizure disorder (AR 40-501 3-31i /
DoDI 6130.03 Vol 2 5.26.c → generally below retention; flying-duty disqualifying) — see the
[TBI hub](index.md) and [seizure page](../seizure/index.md).
