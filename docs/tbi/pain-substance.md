# TBI comorbidity — Pain & substance use

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** chronic pain and substance use are common, **bidirectional** with TBI, and both are
worsened by the reflex reach for **opioids** — keep the plan **opioid-sparing** and screen actively.

!!! orderset "Manage the pain, screen the substances"
    - **Chronic pain** (neck/cervicogenic, musculoskeletal, headache) — **multimodal, opioid-sparing:**
      physical therapy, TCAs/SNRIs/gabapentinoids as appropriate, nerve blocks, and treating the
      **headache phenotype**. **Opioids worsen cognition, mood, medication-overuse headache, and duty
      risk** — avoid them and taper existing use.
    - **Screen for alcohol and substance use** — it's **bidirectional** with TBI (raises injury risk and
      impairs recovery). Screen at intake and follow-up; treat and involve **behavioral health**.
    - **Watch the interactions** — sedatives, opioids, and alcohol compound the cognitive and
      autonomic/exercise-intolerance picture.

*Military note:* opioid/substance issues carry fitness-for-duty, drug-testing, and readiness
implications — an added reason to stay opioid-sparing. See the [TBI hub](index.md).
