# TBI comorbidity — Headache

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**The most common post-TBI symptom.** The rule is **treat the phenotype, start early, and cap
acute-medication days** — PTH is a high-risk medication-overuse setting.

!!! note "Name the phenotype (it drives treatment)"
    - **Migraine-like** — most common (**~90%**); the migraine acute/preventive ladders apply.
    - **Tension-type-like.**
    - **Cervicogenic** — neck-driven; **manual therapy** (2025 AAPM&R Good Practice) and **occipital/
      cervical nerve blocks.**
    - **Occipital neuralgia** — occipital nerve blocks.
    - **Vestibular migraine** — commonly missed; overlaps the **[vestibular/dizziness](vestibular.md)**
      domain — screen for it.

**ICHD-3 framing:** **acute PTH** = onset within **7 days** of injury, resolving by **3 months**;
**persistent PTH** = beyond **3 months**. With a **pre-existing** headache disorder, call it PTH only if
it **clearly worsened** (≈ doubled frequency/severity) in temporal relation to the injury — the
distinction drives escalation and VA/MEB documentation.

- **Acute:** stratified by severity (NSAID → triptan, or a **gepant/lasmiditan** where triptans are
  unsuitable); **prefer a gepant** (no MOH). The **only positive acute-PTH RCT** is **IV metoclopramide
  10 mg + diphenhydramine 25 mg** (n=81; 1.4 pts greater pain relief vs placebo). **Scheduled** (not
  PRN) analgesics in the **first 72 h** post-injury are an emerging way to blunt acute-phase severity.
- **Preventive** (start at **≥8 monthly headache days** if acute therapy is optimized and headaches
  remain disabling — Lancet Neurol 2021; **>10 days/mo or disabling** — 2025 AAPM&R):
  **amitriptyline/nortriptyline** (also helps sleep/mood), **venlafaxine, candesartan, propranolol**;
  **onabotulinumtoxinA** — the **strongest positive PTH-specific RCT** (↓ cumulative headaches/wk 43%
  vs +35% placebo), so consider it **earlier** than "chronic-migraine only." **Nerve blocks** are
  high-yield — consider early.
- **CGRP agents:** used by **extrapolation from migraine** — PTH-specific data are weak. The one
  completed **placebo-controlled CGRP-mAb RCT in PTH (fremanezumab) was negative**; erenumab has only
  open-label support. "Receptor- vs ligand-targeting preferred" is **not** established in PTH.
- **Non-drug:** **headache-specific CBT** (McGeary 2022 RCT in post-9/11 veterans: HIT-6 **−3.4**, and
  **PTSD −6.5** — the strongest non-drug PTH evidence, ideal for this population), aerobic exercise,
  sleep, cervical PT.
- **Emerging — neuromodulation:** FDA-cleared devices (**eTNS, nVNS, REN, sTMS**) for migraine-phenotype
  PTH, useful when **MOH risk** or drug access is limited; small **rTMS/TMS** pilots are promising but
  preliminary.
- **Watch MOH:** cap **analgesics-with-caffeine, triptans, or opioids/butalbital at <10 days/mo**
  (simple analgesics <15); avoid **opioids and butalbital** outright.

**Full detail — doses, the acute/preventive ladders, the CBT and aerobic-exercise evidence, and the
military/TRICARE specifics — are on the dedicated [post-traumatic headache page](../headache/post-traumatic.md).**
