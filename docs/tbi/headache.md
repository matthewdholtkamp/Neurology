# TBI comorbidity — Headache

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**The most common post-TBI symptom.** Persistent post-traumatic headache is usually **migraine-like**
(sometimes tension-type-like) — **treat the phenotype, start early, and cap acute-medication days**
(PTH is a high-risk medication-overuse setting).

- **Acute:** stratified by severity (NSAID → triptan, or a **gepant/lasmiditan** where triptans are
  unsuitable). **Prefer a gepant** (no MOH); the only positive acute-PTH RCT is **metoclopramide +
  diphenhydramine**.
- **Preventive (≥10 headache days/mo or disabling):** **amitriptyline/nortriptyline** (also helps
  sleep/mood), **venlafaxine, candesartan, propranolol**; **onabotulinumtoxinA** (the only positive
  PTH-specific RCT) — consider it earlier than "chronic-migraine only"; CGRP agents (receptor-targeting
  preferred). **Nerve blocks** are high-yield — consider early.
- **Non-drug:** **headache-specific CBT** (also improves PTSD), aerobic exercise, sleep, cervical PT.
- **Avoid opioids and butalbital.**

**Full detail — doses, the acute/preventive ladders, the CBT and aerobic-exercise evidence, and the
military/TRICARE specifics — are on the dedicated [post-traumatic headache page](../headache/post-traumatic.md).**
