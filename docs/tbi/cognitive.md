# TBI comorbidity — Cognitive

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** post-TBI complaints of **attention, processing speed, memory, and executive function**
are common — but **much of it is treatable *contributors*, not fixed damage.** Fix those first, then
rehabilitate what remains.

!!! orderset "Contributors first, then rehab"
    - **Treat the treatable drivers first** — **sleep, mood/PTSD, pain, medication side effects
      (sedatives, anticholinergics, topiramate, opioids), and substances.** A large share of
      "cognitive impairment" resolves when these are addressed.
    - **Cognitive rehabilitation** (speech-language pathology / OT-led) for persistent deficits;
      **compensatory strategies** (calendars, checklists, routines, reduced multitasking).
    - **Stimulants** (e.g., **methylphenidate**) can help post-TBI attention/processing speed in
      selected patients — **specialist-guided**, with duty- and drug-testing implications.
    - **Neuropsychological testing** for persistent, functionally-limiting, or disposition-relevant
      deficits (also documents the MEB/VA picture).

*Military note:* objective **neuropsych findings** carry weight in retention/DES decisions — document
them. See the [TBI hub](index.md).
