# TBI comorbidity — Cognitive

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** post-TBI complaints of **attention, processing speed, memory, and executive function**
are common — but **much of it is treatable *contributors*, not fixed damage.** Fix those first, then
rehabilitate what remains. TRACK-TBI found **13.5% of mTBI** patients had a poor cognitive outcome at
1 year (vs 4.5% of controls), **memory and processing speed** most affected.

!!! note "Impairment profiles are heterogeneous — treat the profile, not a label"
    At 6 months (TRACK-TBI), the dominant deficit in **moderate–severe** TBI was **processing speed
    (42%)**, then **memory (27%)** and **executive function (20%)**; in **mTBI**, processing speed
    (17–19%) and memory (14–17%) predominated. **Global impairment across all domains is uncommon** —
    which argues for **personalized** rehabilitation, not one-size-fits-all. A large cohort (n=15,764)
    found **attention and executive function** are the **most TBI-sensitive** domains (≈2× the effect of
    processing speed/working memory), and that lasting deficits track with **≥3 lifetime mTBIs** — a key
    counseling point for service members continuing high-risk activity.

## Assessment

!!! note "Neuropsychological testing — when, and why"
    - **Not routinely within the first 30 days** (VA/DoD) — first-48-hour deficits usually resolve in
      days–weeks; early testing mislabels normal recovery.
    - **Most useful at >30 days** when symptoms persist and **functional impact needs documenting.**
    - **In the military, it drives disposition:** **RBANS** significantly predicted **return-to-duty vs
      medical retirement** (p=0.001), and **25%** of members cleared on symptom-resolution alone had
      **not** yet achieved **neurocognitive** recovery — so use testing as an **adjunct to symptom-based
      clearance**, not an afterthought.

!!! warning "Performance validity testing (PVT) — include it in *every* evaluation"
    In compensation/litigation contexts, an estimated **~40% of mTBI cases fail effort tests**, which
    **invalidates** the cognitive scores; failing even a **single PVT** predicts significantly lower
    performance. Failure is commoner with **uncomplicated mTBI, compensation-seeking, and longer time
    since injury.** **PVT failure ≠ malingering** — interpret in context (**pain, fatigue, depression,
    medication effects, poor engagement**). This is directly load-bearing for **MEB/VA** ratings, where
    cognitive documentation drives the outcome — a report **without** validity testing is incomplete.

## Manage

!!! orderset "Step 1 — treat the treatable drivers first"
    A large share of "cognitive impairment" resolves once these are addressed — **review all meds at
    every visit** (polypharmacy is the rule in multi-comorbid TBI):

    - **Treat:** **[sleep](sleep.md)**, **[mood/PTSD](mood-ptsd.md)**, **pain**, and **substances.**
    - **Avoid (cognition-toxic):** **benzodiazepines**, **anticholinergics**, **bupropion** and
      **traditional antipsychotics** (seizure threshold), **lithium** (confusion).
    - **Minimize:** **opioids**, **topiramate** (word-finding), **sedating antihistamines**,
      **gabapentin/pregabalin** (sedation).

!!! orderset "Step 2 — non-drug: cognitive rehab + exercise"
    - **Cognitive rehabilitation is clinician-directed** (SLP/OT-led). **Clinician-directed beats
      self-administered computerized "brain training"** — self-directed computer training was **less
      effective and associated with *worse* outcomes** (VA/DoD).
    - **Compensatory strategy instruction has the strongest functional effect** (SMD ~0.58, ASHA 2022) —
      calendars, checklists, routines, reduced multitasking.
    - **Trial 4–6 sessions to confirm benefit;** prolonged treatment without gains fosters a **"sick
      role"** and is discouraged.
    - **Assistive tech should be familiar/commercial** (smartphone apps, alarms, calendars) — custom
      devices have **higher abandonment.**
    - **Early sub-threshold aerobic exercise** aids neuroplasticity and has **cognitive** (not just
      headache) benefit (2025 AAPM&R Good Practice) — see the exercise prescription on the
      [TBI hub](index.md#return-to-activity-duty).

!!! orderset "Step 3 — pharmacotherapy (dosed, specialist-guided)"
    - **Methylphenidate — best-evidenced, domain-specific (2026 ACRM guideline).** Start **5 mg BID**,
      titrate toward ~**0.3 mg/kg BID** (commonly 10–20 mg BID); monitor BP/HR. Usually well tolerated,
      **no increased seizure risk** in the evidence base.
        - **Should be offered** → **sustained attention & processing speed** (d 0.39–0.45).
        - **May be offered** → **selective attention** (d 0.33).
        - **Should NOT be offered** → **executive function or working memory** (no benefit).
        - **Should be offered** → **depressive symptoms** when rapid response and/or cognitive
          impairment are priorities (d 1.06; overlaps [mood/PTSD](mood-ptsd.md)).
        - A 2024 meta-analysis (14 RCTs, n=4,434) confirms a small but real cognitive effect (d 0.34).
    - **Amantadine 100 mg BID** (up to 200 mg BID) — for **moderate–severe TBI in the subacute phase.**
      The **NEJM 2012 RCT (n=184)** showed it **accelerates functional recovery** in disorders of
      consciousness; benefit is **greatest started in the first week for <1 month**, and a 2025
      meta-analysis found it significant **only in the first ~6 weeks** (no long-term advantage). **Not**
      for chronic mTBI.
    - **Donepezil 5 → 10 mg/day** — **emerging**; a 2024 meta-analysis showed a large effect (d 1.68)
      but on only 3 studies, and a small crossover RCT improved short-term memory and sustained
      attention. Insufficient for a guideline recommendation — **specialist-guided** option.

!!! note "Long-term: repeated exposure & dementia risk"
    **mTBI is an emerging dementia risk factor**, and TRACK-TBI shows some patients decline after an
    apparent recovery. Risk concentrates with **repeated exposure (≥3 lifetime mTBIs)** — fold this into
    honest counseling about continuing high-risk activity, and connect it to the **TES/CTE** discussion
    on the [TBI hub](index.md#long-term-cte-neurodegeneration-suicide-risk).

!!! tip "TRICARE — cognitive prescribing"
    **Generic methylphenidate** (**Schedule II** — stimulant/drug-testing rules apply), **amantadine**,
    and **donepezil** are formulary. **Cognitive rehabilitation** (SLP/OT) and **neuropsychological
    testing** go through normal rehab/behavioral-health referral. *Verify at the TRICARE Formulary
    Search.*

*Military note:* objective **neuropsych findings (with PVTs)** carry weight in retention/DES decisions,
and **RBANS** predicts return-to-duty vs medical retirement — document them. See the
[TBI hub](index.md) for the retention/aeromedical framing.
