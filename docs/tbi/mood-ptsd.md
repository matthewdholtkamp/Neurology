# TBI comorbidity — Mood & PTSD

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** depression, anxiety, and **PTSD track tightly with TBI** in service members and drive
outcome more than seizure/symptom counts do — treat them explicitly, and **screen for suicide every
visit.**

!!! orderset "Treat by target"
    - **Depression / anxiety → CBT + an SSRI/SNRI** (sertraline, escitalopram, venlafaxine). Avoid
      highly anticholinergic/sedating agents (worsen cognition).
    - **PTSD → trauma-focused therapy** (cognitive processing therapy / prolonged exposure) ± SSRI.
      **Prazosin** for nightmares — a two-for-one with post-traumatic headache. **Note:**
      **headache-specific CBT** improved *both* the headache and PTSD in veterans, whereas treating
      PTSD alone did **not** fix the headache — so refer for headache-specific CBT, not just PTSD care
      (see the [PTH page](../headache/post-traumatic.md)).
    - **Irritability / aggression** — behavioral strategies; an SSRI; specialist input for severe cases.

!!! danger "Screen for suicidal ideation at every visit"
    TBI **independently raises suicide risk** (amplified by mood/PTSD, chronic pain, and repeated
    injury). Screen, treat the drivers aggressively, restrict means, and connect to behavioral health /
    the **Veterans Crisis Line**. (Also flagged on the [TBI hub](index.md).)
