# TBI comorbidity — Mood & PTSD

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** depression, anxiety, and **PTSD track tightly with TBI** in service members and **drive
outcome more than seizure/symptom counts do** — treat them explicitly, screen with validated tools, and
**screen for suicide every visit.** In military/veteran TBI, **PTSD runs ~36–37%** (≈4× the odds vs
non-TBI military), **depression ~30% in the first year** (~80% of episodes begin within 6 months), and
**anxiety 11–24%.** A 2024 JAMA Netw Open cohort showed both mild and moderate–severe TBI carry a
**decade-long, non-plateauing** excess risk of depression, anxiety, and suicidal ideation/attempt.

!!! danger "Screen for suicidal ideation at every visit"
    TBI **independently raises suicide risk** (amplified by mood/PTSD, chronic pain, and repeated
    injury). Screen (**C-SSRS or PHQ-9 item 9**), treat the drivers aggressively, **restrict means**,
    and connect to behavioral health / the **Veterans Crisis Line (dial 988, then 1)**. (Also flagged on
    the [TBI hub](index.md).)

!!! note "Screen and measure — validated tools, standard cutoffs (2025 AAPM&R, Strong for)"
    Standard primary-care cutoffs apply **without modification** in TBI. Screen **early (~1 month)** and
    **again** in anyone still symptomatic — **peak onset is 2–6 months** post-injury.

    - **Depression** — **PHQ-2** → **PHQ-9.**
    - **Anxiety** — **GAD-2** → **GAD-7.**
    - **PTSD** — **PCL-5.**
    - **Suicidality** — **C-SSRS** or **PHQ-9 item 9.**
    - **Alcohol misuse** — **AUDIT-C.**

## Depression & anxiety

!!! orderset "Treat by target — CBT + an antidepressant (dosed)"
    - **Psychotherapy:** CBT / behavioral activation is first-line and combines well with medication.
    - **SSRI/SNRI** (depression evidence base is broad): **sertraline 50 mg/day → 50–200**,
      **escitalopram 10 → 20 mg**, or **venlafaxine XR 75 → 225 mg.** Avoid highly
      **anticholinergic/sedating** agents (worsen cognition).
    - **Methylphenidate — strongest *TBI-specific* evidence for depressive symptoms.** The **2026 ACRM
      practice guideline (Should be offered)** recommends it when **rapid response** and/or
      **co-occurring cognitive impairment** are priorities: **start 5 mg BID**, titrate toward
      ~**0.3 mg/kg BID** (commonly 10–20 mg BID); monitor BP/HR. (Overlaps the
      [cognitive](cognitive.md) domain.)
    - **Early prevention (not yet guideline-endorsed):** **sertraline 50 mg/day** started soon after TBI
      reduced incident depression (**NNT ~6** in one RCT).

## PTSD

!!! orderset "PTSD — trauma-focused therapy first (VA/DoD 2023, Strong for)"
    **TBI does not preclude trauma-focused therapy** — these work in comorbid TBI. Offer a first-line
    trauma-focused psychotherapy:

    - **Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT)**, **Prolonged Exposure (PE)**, or **EMDR.**
    - **Written Exposure Therapy (WET)** — 2025 APA-suggested alternative: **fewer sessions, no
      homework, lower dropout** — feasible in primary care / deployed settings. A 2025 JAMA Psychiatry
      RCT (n=700) found **WET and SSRIs comparably effective** in primary care; for SSRI non-responders,
      **switching to venlafaxine beat WET augmentation.**

!!! orderset "PTSD — pharmacotherapy (dosed)"
    Guideline-recommended agents are **specific** — not all SSRIs qualify:

    - **First-line (VA/DoD 2023, Strong for): paroxetine 20 → 60 mg**, **sertraline 50 → 200 mg**, or
      **venlafaxine XR 75 → 225 mg.** **Escitalopram is *not* PTSD-recommended** (no PTSD-specific
      evidence), and **fluoxetine was dropped** from the recommended list on updated evidence.
    - **Prazosin — nuance the evidence.** The large **PACT trial (NEJM 2018, n=304 veterans) was
      negative** for nightmares/PTSD at 26 weeks. But a 2025 meta-analysis (10 RCTs, n=648) found
      prazosin **improves nightmares (SMD −0.64) and insomnia (SMD −0.65)** — **not overall PTSD.**
      VA/DoD **suggests against** it for overall PTSD but gives a **Weak for** specifically for
      **nightmares/sleep.** Use it targeted: **1 mg qhs, titrate slowly (to ~5–15 mg)** by
      **orthostatic-BP tolerance** — a **two-for-one** with post-traumatic headache.

## Irritability / aggression

!!! orderset "Post-TBI agitation/aggression (dosed) — and what to avoid"
    - **Amantadine 100 mg BID** — **best TBI-specific evidence** (multiple RCTs) for irritability/
      aggression.
    - **Beta-blockers** (propranolol, pindolol) — reduce the **intensity** of agitation (titrate from a
      low dose as BP/HR allow).
    - **Methylphenidate** — improves anger measures (dose as above), doubles for depression/cognition.
    - **Avoid haloperidol** — may **hinder neurocognitive recovery and prolong post-traumatic amnesia.**
    - **Avoid benzodiazepines** for agitation — cognitive impairment and **paradoxical disinhibition.**

## Emerging & adjunctive

!!! note "Stellate ganglion block (SGB) — relevant to the military population"
    A local-anesthetic block of the cervical sympathetic chain. A **2020 JAMA Psychiatry RCT (n=113
    active-duty)** found **two right-sided SGBs cut CAPS-5 by −12.2 points vs sham**; a 2026 VA series
    (n=579) reported **68% clinically meaningful improvement at 1 week.** **Hyperarousal symptoms
    respond best**, effects last **~3–6 months**, and it can be **repeated.** The **VA actively offers
    SGB** — reasonable **adjunct** for refractory hyperarousal.

!!! warning "MDMA-assisted therapy — not FDA-approved"
    The **FDA declined to approve** MDMA-assisted psychotherapy for PTSD in **August 2024** (trial-
    validity, expectancy, and functional-unblinding concerns); an **additional Phase 3 trial** is
    required. Both the **2025 APA** and **2023 VA/DoD** CPGs rate psychedelics as **insufficient
    evidence** to recommend for or against — don't adopt prematurely.

!!! note "Moral injury — distinct from PTSD (a military-specific concept)"
    Distress from **acts (or failures to act) that violate one's moral code** — presenting with
    **shame, guilt, and existential distress** rather than fear-based symptoms. It **may not respond**
    to standard trauma-focused therapies built around **fear extinction.** Emerging treatments include
    **Adaptive Disclosure** and **Impact of Killing.** Recognize it; screen when the narrative is about
    transgression, not threat.

!!! tip "Treat the domains together — they're bidirectional"
    - **SSRIs/SNRIs also reduce headache frequency** (2025 AAPM&R) — a **dual-purpose** choice when
      mood **and** [headache](headache.md) coexist.
    - **Treating [sleep](sleep.md)** (CBT-I; prazosin for nightmares) substantially improves mental
      health — treat concurrently or prioritize by severity.
    - **Refer for *headache-specific* CBT**, not just PTSD care: it improved **both** the headache and
      PTSD in veterans (McGeary 2022), whereas treating PTSD alone did **not** fix the headache (see the
      [PTH page](../headache/post-traumatic.md)).

!!! tip "TRICARE — mood/PTSD prescribing"
    **Sertraline, paroxetine, escitalopram, venlafaxine, prazosin, amantadine, propranolol**, and
    **generic methylphenidate** (**Schedule II** — stimulant rules apply) are formulary/generic.
    **CPT/PE/EMDR/WET**, and **SGB** go through behavioral-health / pain or anesthesia referral. *Verify
    at the TRICARE Formulary Search.*

*Military note:* PTSD, depression, and **suicide risk** are central to deployability, profile, and
MEB/DES decisions — document screening, diagnosis, treatment trials, and response. See the
[TBI hub](index.md) for the retention/aeromedical framing.
