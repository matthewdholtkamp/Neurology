# TBI comorbidity — Autonomic

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** post-TBI **dysautonomia** — orthostatic and **exercise intolerance**, palpitations,
temperature dysregulation — overlaps the "can't tolerate exertion" concussion picture and is very
treatable with graded reconditioning.

!!! orderset "Recognize and manage"
    - **Symptoms:** orthostatic intolerance/lightheadedness, **exertional symptom exacerbation**,
      palpitations/tachycardia, sweating and temperature dysregulation.
    - **Assess:** orthostatic vitals (consider **POTS**: sustained HR rise ≥30 bpm on standing without
      orthostatic hypotension); overlaps the autonomic/exercise-intolerance side of concussion.
    - **Manage:** the **[sub-threshold aerobic-exercise reconditioning protocol](../headache/post-traumatic.md)**
      (dose to a heart-rate/symptom threshold), **hydration + salt**, gradual upright tolerance,
      compression; treat **orthostatic hypotension / POTS** if present (fluids, salt, midodrine/
      fludrocortisone in refractory cases).
    - **Distinct entity:** acute **paroxysmal sympathetic hyperactivity** ("sympathetic storming") is a
      **severe-TBI ICU** problem — different setting and management.

*Military note:* exercise intolerance directly limits return-to-duty fitness — the reconditioning
protocol doubles as a documentable progression. See the [TBI hub](index.md).
