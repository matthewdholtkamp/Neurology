# TBI comorbidity — Vision / oculomotor

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** **convergence insufficiency and oculomotor dysfunction** are common after TBI and drive
reading difficulty, headache, and "screen intolerance" — screen for them, because **vision/oculomotor
therapy helps.**

!!! orderset "Screen and treat"
    - **Screen the oculomotor system** — **near point of convergence**, smooth pursuit and saccades,
      accommodation; a **VOMS** (vestibular/ocular-motor screen) catches most of it.
    - **Convergence insufficiency / oculomotor dysfunction → vision therapy / oculomotor rehab**
      (optometry/neuro-optometry).
    - **Photophobia** — tinted (FL-41) lenses and graded light exposure; treat the **migraine
      phenotype** that's usually driving it.
    - **Refer to neuro-ophthalmology / optometry** for persistent **diplopia**, a fixed deficit, or an
      afferent problem — and exclude a cranial-nerve or structural cause (see the
      [diplopia page](../diplopia/index.md)).

*Military note:* aircrew and other visually-demanding duties are especially sensitive to persistent
oculomotor deficits — see the [TBI hub](index.md).
