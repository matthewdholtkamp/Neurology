# TBI comorbidity — Vision / oculomotor

<span class="reviewed-stamp">Last reviewed: July 2026</span>

*Part of the [TBI work-up & comorbidities](index.md) hub.*

**One-liner:** **convergence insufficiency and oculomotor dysfunction** are common after TBI and drive
reading difficulty, headache, and "screen intolerance" — screen routinely, because **office-based
vision therapy has now-strong (Level 1) evidence.** Convergence insufficiency runs **~36%** and
accommodative dysfunction **~43%** after TBI, visual-field loss **~18%** overall; **69%** of persisting
concussions have **≥1** vision disorder, and **>40%** of blast-TBI veterans report diplopia.

## Name the diagnosis — it's a spectrum

!!! note "Post-TBI visual/oculomotor differential (2022 AAN/NANOS taxonomy)"
    - **Convergence insufficiency (CI)** — the classic; reduced near convergence → near blur, diplopia,
      reading fatigue.
    - **Accommodative insufficiency (AI)** — as common as CI (~43%); blur/headache/fatigue with near
      work; **the near triad (accommodation–convergence–miosis) is linked**, so AI and CI usually
      **coexist.**
    - **Saccadic dysfunction** — impaired rapid refixation → slow, inaccurate **reading** (horizontal
      *and* vertical).
    - **Smooth-pursuit dysfunction** — poor tracking → motion sensitivity, trouble following
      lectures/conversation.
    - **Visual-field loss** — **~18%** overall, far higher in **moderate–severe (40%) than mTBI (7%).**
    - **Cranial-nerve palsies** — **CN IV (trochlear) is the most susceptible to minor trauma**; CN III
      and VI less so; presents as **binocular diplopia.**
    - **Higher-order visual-cognitive dysfunction** — visual-spatial processing, spatial bias, visual
      attention — **mimics** an ocular problem; differentiate with **OT vision / behavioral-neurology**
      assessment.

## Assess

!!! note "Screening & bedside tools (with cutoffs)"
    Start with the **VA/DoD basic PCP eye exam** — **near *and* distance acuity**, confrontation fields,
    pupils, eye movements, external exam, nystagmus — before specialist referral.

    - **VOMS** — 5 domains (smooth pursuit, horizontal/vertical saccades, NPC, horizontal VOR, visual-
      motion sensitivity). **Abnormal = symptom provocation ≥2 points on any item, or NPC >5 cm**
      (AUC 0.85–0.89).
    - **King-Devick** — rapid number-naming (saccadic speed), **<1 min**, sensitivity ~81%; **any
      worsening from baseline is abnormal** — adds the vision piece SCAT6/MACE2 miss.
    - **Near point of convergence (NPC)** — target to the nose; **normal ≤5 cm**, higher suggests CI.
    - **Accommodative amplitude** — push-up or minus-lens vs age-expected norm (Hofstetter); reduced =
      AI.
    - **Near visual acuity** — test **separately** from distance (commonly overlooked).

!!! danger "Urgent eye-care referral — red flags (VA/DoD)"
    **Vision loss or decline, new diplopia, abnormal pupils, abnormal external eye exam, abnormal visual
    behavior** (unexpectedly bumping into things), **nystagmus**, or **acute ocular symptoms** (severe
    eye pain, flashes/floaters, severe photophobia) → **urgent** neuro-ophthalmology/ophthalmology.
    Exclude a cranial-nerve or structural cause — localize the palsy (CN III, IV, VI) before
    attributing diplopia to convergence insufficiency or accommodative dysfunction.

## Treat

!!! orderset "Convergence / accommodative insufficiency — office-based therapy (Level 1)"
    - **Refer for office-based vergence/accommodative therapy with movement (OBVAM)** — the **CONCUSS
      RCT (BJSM 2026)**, the first randomized trial for **concussion-related CI**, found **88% success
      with immediate therapy vs 8% delayed** at 6 weeks (NPC improved 7.9 vs 1.8 cm; PFV +17.5Δ vs
      2.5Δ; **79% symptom improvement vs 13%**), and a secondary analysis showed **86% resolution of
      accommodative insufficiency vs 28%.** **Refer early** — immediate therapy shortens the
      symptomatic period and speeds return to activity.
    - **Evidence hierarchy — prescribe the right thing:**
        - **Office-based** vergence/accommodative therapy is **best** (CITT high-certainty; CONCUSS
          extends it to concussion CI).
        - **Home pencil push-ups alone = placebo** — a common misconception; **do not** prescribe as
          standalone treatment.
        - **Saccade and pursuit training** have **insufficient** evidence; there is **no rationale** for
          **binasal occlusion, yoked prisms, or monocular vertical prisms** (AAN/NANOS 2022).
        - **Trial, then reassess** — VA/DoD rates the evidence low and cautions against **prolonged**
          therapy that fosters a "sick role."

!!! orderset "Photophobia — relieve, but don't lock in dark adaptation"
    - **FL-41 or gray-tinted lenses** for symptomatic relief (both improve light tolerance; 2026 cohort).
    - **But** prolonged tinted-lens use **may impede long-term adaptation** — 1-year light-intolerance
      recovery was **less** likely in tinted-lens wearers (AAN/NANOS 2022). **Pair with graded light
      exposure; avoid indefinite dark-adaptation behavior.**
    - **Treat the underlying migraine phenotype** — individualized spectral filters in a military cohort
      (n=392) cut monthly migraines **14.8 → 1.9** with **74%** migraine-free and **>70%** less
      medication (see [headache](headache.md)).

!!! orderset "Diplopia / strabismus — prisms first"
    - For **small-angle strabismus or decompensated phorias**, **prism glasses give immediate relief**
      and are **first-line** before surgery or vision therapy.
    - **Fresnel prisms** are a good **temporary** measure while monitoring for spontaneous recovery —
      **traumatic CN palsies often improve over 3–6 months.** Persistent/large-angle or afferent
      problems → neuro-ophthalmology.

!!! tip "Vision, vestibular, and headache overlap — treat them together"
    Oculomotor deficits and symptom severity track with **poorer dynamic mobility** — a
    **vision–vestibular–balance triad** that benefits from **coordinated multimodal** rehab. The
    **VOMS** deliberately spans both vestibular and oculomotor domains because they **can't be cleanly
    separated** (see [vestibular](vestibular.md)).

*Military note:* aircrew and other visually-demanding duties are especially sensitive to persistent
oculomotor deficits; document objective findings (NPC, VOMS, King-Devick, fields) for the
[TBI hub](index.md) retention/aeromedical framing.
